WELCOME TO KOSGODA TURTLE HATCHERY

About hatchery

The Kosgoda Turtle Hatchery, also known as the Kosgoda Sea Turtle Conservation Project, is a well-known turtle conservation initiative located in Kosgoda, Sri Lanka. The hatchery is dedicated to protecting sea turtles and their eggs from various threats and increasing their chances of survival.

More about Kosgoda turtle conservation project

Objectives

The primary objectives of the Kosgoda Turtle Hatchery include protecting sea turtles, conserving their nesting habitats, promoting public awareness about the importance of turtle conservation, and conducting research to enhance turtle survival rates.

Conservation effort

The hatchery actively collects sea turtle eggs from vulnerable nesting sites along the Sri Lankan coastline to prevent them from being destroyed or harvested illegally. The collected eggs are then carefully incubated in specially constructed sand-filled hatcheries within the facility. Once hatched, the baby turtles are released into the ocean during the safer nighttime hours to minimize predation.

Turtle species

The Kosgoda Turtle Hatchery primarily focuses on five species of sea turtles found in Sri Lanka:

  • Green turtle
  • Loggerhead turtle
  • Hawksbill turtle
  • Olive ridley turtle
  • Leatherback turtle

Visitor experience

The hatchery welcomes visitors and provides educational tours to raise awareness about sea turtle conservation. Visitors can learn about the life cycle of sea turtles, witness turtle hatchlings, and even participate in releasing them into the sea. These activities aim to inspire individuals to contribute to the protection of sea turtles and their habitats.

Community involvement

The Kosgoda Turtle Hatchery actively involves the local community, including fishermen, by raising awareness about sustainable fishing practices and the importance of protecting sea turtles. By engaging with the community, the hatchery aims to foster a sense of stewardship among the locals and promote a harmonious coexistence between humans and sea turtles.

Additional initiatives

In addition to its primary conservation efforts, the hatchery may also engage in rescue and rehabilitation of injured sea turtles, as well as research activities to better understand the behavior, biology, and conservation needs of sea turtles.
